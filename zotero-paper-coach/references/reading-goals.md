# Reading goals

Use this file to align annotations with the reader’s actual job.

## beginner-learning

Reader need:
- understand what the paper is asking
- understand what the authors found
- avoid getting lost in jargon

Favor:
- green orientation highlights
- yellow conclusion highlights
- mentor-style `[人话]` notes
- page-level reading order suggestions

## quick-read

Reader need:
- get the thesis fast
- extract only the high-value points

Favor:
- abstract and conclusion anchors
- strongest result sentences
- one note on limitation

## exam-prep

Reader need:
- remember structure and high-yield content
- answer likely oral or written questions

Favor:
- definitions
- contribution statements
- compare/contrast passages
- `[复述]` notes

## literature-review

Reader need:
- position the paper in a field
- cite it responsibly

Favor:
- gap statements
- novelty claims
- strongest evidence
- caveats and scope boundaries
- quotable summary lines

## replication

Reader need:
- reproduce the procedure or critique reproducibility

Favor:
- data source details
- parameter/configuration statements
- metric definitions
- decoder, auxiliary model, and implementation caveats

## citation-harvest

Reader need:
- collect reusable claims without overclaiming

Favor:
- scoped conclusions
- clearly bounded result statements
- explicit limitation sentences that prevent misuse
